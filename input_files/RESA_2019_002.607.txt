CONSTITUTION DE SOCIETE
du vingt-sept décembre 2018

 

The undersigned, Mr. Manh Duy Van CAO, a citizen of the United Kingdom, born on
24 December 1975 in Saigon, Vietnam, with personal address at 5 Firs Avenue, London
N10 3LY, United Kingdom (the ”Founder"), hereby incorporates a cooperative
company organised as a public limited liability company (société coopérative organisée
comme une société anonyme) under the laws of Luxembourg, governed by the
following articles of association:

”ARTICLES OF ASSOCIATION

Article 1. Name and corporate form.

1.1 The name of the company (the ”Company") is ”Stafford Sustainable
Secondaries Fund Ill GP Lux, CoopSA".

1.2 The Company is a cooperative company organised as a public limited liability
(société coopérative organisée comme une société anonyme) subject to the

provisions of the Luxembourg law on commercial companies dated 10 August
1915, as amended (the ”Company Law").

Article 2. Registered office.

2.1 The registered office of the Company is established in the City of Luxembourg.

2.2 The registered office may be transferred to any other place within the Grand
Duchy of Luxembourg by a decision of its board of directors or its sole director,
or through a resolution of the shareholder(s) of the Company passed in
accordance with the Luxembourg Law.

2.3 The Company may have offices and branches, both in the Grand Duchy of
Luxembourg and abroad.

Article 3. Corporate object.
3.1 The corporate object of the Company is to act, either as a general partner,
unlimited shareholder, and/or as a manager or director of one or several

limited partnerships, incorporated in the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg or
abroad.


3.2 The corporate object of the Company is also to acquire participations in
companies and undertakings of whatever form, in Luxembourg and abroad,
and to manage the same as well as to do all that is connected therewith or may
be conducive thereto, all to be interpreted in the broadest sense. The object
of the Company includes the participation in the creation, development,
management and control of any company or undertaking. lt includes also the
following: acquiring, by subscription, purchase, exchange or in any other
manner, any stock, shares and other equity securities, debentures, certificates
of deposit and other debt instruments and, more generally, any claims against
and securities and financial instruments issued by any public or private body.

3.3 The Company may furthermore borrow in any form and finance its subsidiaries
and other group companies as well as third parties and it may give guarantees
and provide security for its own obligations as well as those of group
companies and third parties, including by pledging or otherwise encumbering
its assets.

Article 4. Duration.
The Company is incorporated for an indefinite term and shall not be dissolved
in any of the instances mentioned in article 1865 of the Civil Code and the
Company shall be continued in each such instance.

Article 5. Share capital.

5.1 The minimum amount of the share capital of the Company is set at twelve
thousand euros (EUR 12,000.00), divided into twelve thousand (12,000) shares
in registered form with a nominal value of one euro (EUR 1.00) each.

5.2 Additional shares can be issued against contributions in cash, in kind or in the
form of services, and will also have a nominal value of one euro (EUR 1.00)
each.

5.3 Each share of the Company is immediately subscribed by the subscriber, and
must be fully paid within three (3) months further to its issuance.

Article 6. Members.

6.1 Every holder of one or more shares in the Company is, as such, a member of
the Company and owes the Company all that which she/he/it has promised to
contribute to the Company.

6.2 The terms of withdrawal, expulsion and admission of members and of
recovering payments made to the Company can be fixed by applicable
provisions of these articles or can be adopted by decisions of the general
meeting.

6.3 The members are not liable for the debts and obligations of the Company.


Article 7. Directors.

The Company is managed by at least three directors. By exception, in the case
where the Company has a sole shareholder, the appointment of at least one
director is sufficient. In case of plurality of directors, they shall together form
a board of directors, which board may exercise all powers not reserved by law
or these articles to the general meeting of shareholders, or to any other body
of the Company.

Article 8. Decision-making by directors.

8.1

8.2

8.3

8.4

A board of directors may validly deliberate in the presence of a majority of
directors. Every decision put to the vote of the board of directors shall be
decided by a majority of votes cast on the decision. No one shall be entitled to
a casting vote.

One or more directors may participate in a meeting by means of a conference
call or by any similar means of communication enabling thus several persons
participating therein to simultaneously communicate with each other. Such
participation shall be deemed equal to a physical presence at the meeting.
Such a decision may be documented in a single document or in several
separate documents having the same content signed by all the members
having participated.

Decisions of the board of directors may at all times be taken without holding a
meeting. A board decision is taken in accordance with this Article 8.3 when all
directors indicate to each other in writing that they share a common view on
a matter. Such a decision may take the form of a written resolution, copies of
which have been signed by each director or to which each director has
otherwise indicated agreement in writing.

In the case of a sole director, the latter shall be entitled to approve any
decision through a written resolution.

Article 9. Representation.

9.1

9.2

The board ofdirectors (or the sole director) represents and binds the Company
towards third parties and at law.

The Company shall be bound toward third parties by two directors acting
jointly and they shall have signing authority accordingly. The sole director may
represent the Company by her/his/its sole signature.

Article 10. General meetings.

10.1

General meetings shall be held at the registered office or at any other place
within the municipality of the registered office as specified in the notice.


10.2 Except where the Company Law otherwise provides, a general meeting must
be called by the board of directors or the sole director, through a notice to be
sent at least seven (7) calendar days before the holding of the meeting,
excluding the day of the meeting and the day on which the notice is given, by
mail, email or by any means accepted by the shareholders. A general meeting
may be called by shorter notice than that otherwise required if shorter notice
is unanimously agreed by the shareholders. The shareholders may decide at
unanimity to waive their notice right.

10.3 In the case of a sole shareholder, the latter shall decide on all decisions within
the scope of the general meeting of the shareholders.

Article 11. Voting and adoption of resolutions.

11.1 Each share entitles the holder thereof to one vote.

11.2 Except where the Company Law or the present articles otherwise provide,
every shareholders' resolution put to a vote shall be decided by a majority of
votes cast on the resolution. In case of an equality of votes, the chair of the
meeting shall not be entitled to a casting vote.

11.3 It is referred to the provisions of the Company Law for all matters of quorum
and majority rules applicable to the general meetings of shareholders.

Article 12. Financial year
The Company’s financial year starts on lst January and ends on the 3lst
December of each year provided that, as a transitional measure, the first
financial year of the Company starts on the date of its incorporation and ends
on 31 December 2019 (all dates inclusive).

Article 13. Profits.

13.1 Subject to Article 13.3, the annual net profit is at the disposal of the general
meeting or the sole shareholder.

13.2 Each share confers an equal right to share in the profits and the liquidation
proceeds of the Company.

13.3 Each financial year, the Company must transfer an amount equal to five
percent (5%) of its net profit to a legal reserve account until such reserve
reaches an amount corresponding to ten percent (10%) of the share capital."

Further to the above, the undersigned, exercising the powers reserved for and vested

in the general meeting as acting as the sole shareholder, declared:

1. Twelve thousand (12,000) shares numbered 1 to 12,000 are hereby issued
with a nominal value of one euro (EUR 1.00) and entirely subscribed for by


the Founder. The whole nominal value of the shares remains unpaid for the

time being.

2. The registered office of the Company is established at 412F route d'Esch, L-
2086 Luxembourg.

3. The first financial year ofthe Company starts on the date hereof and ends on
31 December 2019.

4. The Founder decides to appoint Mr. Riccardo Zorzetto, a citizen of Italy, born

on 16 May 1981 in Caprino Veronese, Italy, with professional address at 412F
route d’Esch, L-2086 Luxembourg, Grand Duchy of Luxembourg, as director
of the Company, to hold office until the conclusion of the Company's first
annual general meeting.

5. The Founder decides to appoint Audit Central S.à r.l., a Luxembourg
company having its registered office at 10b, rue des Mérovingiens, L-8070
Bertrange, Grand Duchy of Luxembourg, represented by Mr. Helmut
Havenith, a Belgian citizen born on 11 December 1963 in Eupen, with
professional address at 10b, rue des Mérovingiens, L-8070 Bertrange, Grand-
Duchy of Luxembourg, as statutory auditor of the Company, to hold office
until the conclusion of the Company's first annual general meeting.

This deed is drawn up in English followed by a version in French. In case of any
discrepancy between the English version and the French version, the English version
shall prevail.

Article 3. Objet social.

3.1

3.2

La Société a pour objet d'agir en tant qu'associé commandité, general partner,

La Société a pour objet la prise de participations dans toutes sociétés et
entreprises sous quelque forme que ce soit, tant au Luxembourg qu'à

et plus généralement de toutes créances sur tout entité publique ou privée et
de valeurs mobilières et instruments financiers émis par toute entité publique
ou privée.

La Société peut, par ailleurs, emprunter sous quelque forme que ce soit et
financer ses filiales et autres sociétés du groupe ainsi que des tiers. Elle peut
également consentir des sûretés ou des garanties, se porter caution tant pour
ses propres obligations que pour celles d'autres sociétés du groupe ou de tiers,
y compris en gageant ou en grevant de quelque manière que ce soit ses actifs.

Article 4. Durée.


La Société est constituée pour une durée illimitée. La Société n'est pas dissoute
dans les cas mentionnés dans l'article 1865 du Code civil et la Société
continuerait d'exister dans chacun de ces cas.

Article 5. Capital social.

5.1 Le capital social minimum de la Société s'élève à douze mille euros
(12.000,00 EUR) et se divise en douze mille (12.000) actions nominatives d'une
valeur nominale d'un euro (1,00 EUR) chacune.

5.2 Des actions additionnelles peuvent être émises en contrepartie d'apports en
numéraire, en nature ou en industrie et auront également une valeur
nominale d'un euro (1,00 EUR) chacune.

5.3 Chaque action de la Société est immédiatement souscrite par le souscripteur
et doit être entièrement payée dans les 3 (trois) mois suivant l'émission.

Article 6. Associés.

6.1 Chaque détenteur d'une ou plusieurs actions de la Société est, à ce titre, un
associé de la Société et est débiteur envers la Société de tout ce qu'il a promis
d'y apporter.

6.2 Les conditions d'admission, de démission et d'exclusion des associés et les
conditions de retrait de versements à la Société sont fixées par ces statuts ou
pourront être adoptées par décisions prises lors d'assemblées générales.

6.3 Les associés ne sont pas tenus des dettes et obligations sociales.

Article 7. Administrateurs.
La Société est gérée par au moins trois administrateurs. Par exception, lorsque
la Société est détenue par un seul associé, la nomination d'un seul
administrateur est suffisante. Lorsque la Société est administrée par plusieurs
administrateurs, ces derniers constitueront un conseil d'administration, lequel
peut exercer tous les pouvoirs non dévolus parla loi ou les présents statuts à
l'assemblée générale des associés ou à un autre organe de la Société.

exercice social de la Société qui commence à la date de constitution de la
Société et finit le 31 décembre 2019 (toutes les dates étant inclusives).
